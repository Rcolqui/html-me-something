Easy and delicious meals for busy nights

We've all had those nights where we dread the thought of having to cook dinner, whether it's because you had a long day or you simply don't feel like cooking. For most people getting take out is the answer, but what if you're feeling burnt out on all your local take out options, or maybe you're trying to save money by eating out less? Not to worry because listed below are a few easy to prepare meals that are not only delicious but you probably have most of the ingredients already!

Fiesta Chicken

Throw all the ingredients in a pot and let it cook. It really doesn't get much more hands off than this right? Slow cooker recipes are the pinnacle of easy and delicious meals and this Fiesta Chicken recipe is no exception. Fiesta Chicken is a great combination of Mexican flavors with chicken, black beans, and corn. Make burritos, or use it on nachos, salads, or rice. Regardless of how you serve it, this easy to make meal is full of flavor and sure to please everyone!

Recipe can be found here: Fiesta Chicken

Don't want to slow cook it? Not a problem! This Fiesta Chicken recipe is easy to make in a multi-purpose pressure cooker for when you're short on time. Just pressure cook it on high for 8 minutes plus 10 minutes natural release if your chicken breasts are thawed, or 14 minutes on high plus 10 minutes natural release if your chicken breasts are frozen. At the end when you add your sour cream/cream cheese, just set your pressure cooker to saute till heated through.

Korean Ground Beef

Korean Ground beef is the quick weeknight version of your favorite Korean restaurant. A plate of sweet and spicy beef made easier with the use of ground beef instead of short ribs. From start to finish, this recipe takes less than 30 minutes to make with a good chunk of that time being used for simmering. What makes this recipe even better is you probably already have most of the ingredients for it on hand. Doesn't get much easier right?

Recipe can be found here: Korean Ground Beef

Want a great side dish to go with this easy to prepare meal? Try this Pan-fried Broccoli recipe from Tasty. It's so easy that you can have it ready by the time your ground beef is done simmering!

Bánh Mì Rice Bowls

This is another slow cooker recipe and one of my favorites to boot. This recipe takes the popular Vietnamese bánh mì sandwich and turns it into a rice bowl. Being quick and easy to prepare along with the flavor combination of the savory pork, pickled carrots, shredded cabbage, and sliced cucumbers, this is a recipe you don't want to miss out on!

Recipe can be found here: Bánh Mì Rice Bowls

Don't want to slow cook it? Not a problem! Cook the pork in a multi-purpose pressure cooker to have this recipe ready in a fraction of the time. Just add all the pork ingredients to your pressure cooker and select the meat/stew function and set the time to "more". You'll have pork that's falling apart in no time!
